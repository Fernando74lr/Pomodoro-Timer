What is this?
Pomodoro Timer

Is an easy way to organize your time for work using a looping pomodoro timer to boost your efficiency.

Technique

The Pomodoro Technique is a method to improve the administration of the time dedicated to an activity.

It was developed by Francesco Cirillo in the late 1980s. A timer is used to divide time into indivisible intervals, called pomodoros, of 25 minutes of activity, followed by 5 minutes of rest, with longer pauses every four pomodoros.

Instructions
1.- Choose a task to be accomplished in simple to-do list or kanban mode (click the "To-Do List" title to switch).
2.- Start the Pomodoro in 25 minutes.
3.- Work focused on the task until the Pomodoro rings, then put a check on your to-do list or kanban mode if you moved forward.
4.- Take a short break (5 minutes is nice).
5.- Go back to work.
6.- Every 4 Pomodoros take a longer break (10/15 min) and restart the cycle.
